e-Pay Service

The e-Pay service allows the user to purchase museum products from anywhere. Whether it be their mobile device, computer or the museum's own tablet screens.

Requirements to be able to use the service
The user will need to have a debit/credit card in order to fulfill a transaction using the e-Pay service

Two ways of paying
The user can either pay contactless or manually by entering their card credentials.

  • Contactless payment

    This method is only available for the museum's tablet screens!
    At any of these museum tablet screens, simply put your card close to the 'contactless' icon and that's it!

  • Manual Payment

    This method is universal and is available on all devices!
    The user is required to fill in 4 mandatory fields that identify their debit/card card:

    • Card Holder

      The full name displayed on the front of the card

    • Card Number

      The 4 pairs of 4-digit numbers displayed on the front of the card

    • Expiration Date

      The date (MM/YY) displayed in the front of the card (e.g. 04/28 or 12/26)

    • CVC

      The 3-digit security code that is on the back of the card

    After filling in these fields, the user can click "Finalize Payment". The service will then validate the card credentials and if valid, will present the user with a "Success" message!

Frequently Asked Questions

I have entered my credentials correctly, yet the payment is stuck on "processing" or it says "failed".

If that is the case, then the problem lies with your card's bank. You thus will have to contact your bank for more information on this issue.

Are my card details/credentials stored anywhere? Is there a security risk?

Absolutely not, to both questions. Firstly, our museum's network is highly encrypted and firewalled. Secondly, your card details are momentarily used for validation and to reach your bank and then are forgotten by the system.
All in all, there is zero security risk. Even if there was a network breach, we keep no information on you so your data will be completely unharmed.
